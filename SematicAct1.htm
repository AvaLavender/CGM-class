What are cats

A "cat" can refer to a domesticated mammal of the family Felidae, a variety of other wild felines like lions and tigers, or an acronym for several different things, including the Common Admission Test (CAT) for graduate programs, the Central Administrative Tribunal (CAT), or Caterpillar heavy equipment. The term is also used in idioms, such as "let the cat out of the bag".

As a domestic animal A small, carnivorous mammal kept as a pet, known for its four legs, tail, and claws. The domestic cat is the only fully domesticated species of the family Felidae. A group of cats is sometimes called a "clowder". As a broader animal group Can refer to any member of the cat family (Felidae), including large cats like lions, tigers, and leopards. These large cats are in the subfamily Pantherinae and can roar, while domestic cats are in the subfamily Felinae and purr.
